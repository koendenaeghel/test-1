<h2><font size="4"><em><a href="https://www.koendenaeghel.be/wiskundeplan.htm" target="_blank" name="">Wiskundeplan</a></em></font></h2>

Op dinsdag 23 mei 2023 werd een 
<b><font color="#ff0000">alternatief leerplan wiskunde</font></b>
gelanceerd: <em><a href="https://www.uitwiskeling.be/author/vlel/?v=d3dcf429c679" target="_blank" name="">Els Vanlommel</a></em>, <em><a href="https://be.linkedin.com/in/pedrotytgat" target="_blank" name="">Pedro Tytgat</a></em>, Koen De Naeghel en <em><a href="https://be.linkedin.com/in/luc-gheysens-aa98112a"_blank" name="">Luc Gheysens</a></em> bieden een concreet en coherent pakket aan voor leerlingen uit de tweede en derde graad middelbaar onderwijs die sterk zijn in wiskunde.  
<p></p>
Alle informatie over dit eigen leerplan vind je op de website <em><a href="http://www.wiskundeplan.be" target="_blank" name="">www.wiskundeplan.be</a></em>.

It turns out that, as in the commutative case, they are related to Castelnuovo functions. From this we obtain an intrinsic proof that the space of torsion free rank one modules on a non-commutative projective plane is connected. In general, the precise inclusion relation between the closures of the strata is still unknown. Guerimand studied this problem for strata whose Hilbert series are as close as possible. Preimposing a certain technical condition he obtained necessary and sufficient conditions for the incidence of such strata. In this paper we present a new approach, based on deformation theory, to Guerimand's result. This allows us to show that the technical condition is not necessary.

We also determine the possible Hilbert functions of these ideals. These results are obtained by using similar methods as for quadratic Artin-Schelter algebras. In particular our results apply to the enveloping algebra of the Heisenberg-Lie algebra from which we deduce a classification of right ideals of an invariant ring of the first Weyl algebra.

<h2><font size="2">Nota's</font></h2>
<p></p>
<ol start="7">
   <li>
      K. De Naeghel and N. Marconnet, <a href="http://www.freewebs.com/koendenaeghel/chess.pdf" target="_blank" name=""><em>An inequality on broken chessboards</em></a>. <p><strong><img style="PADDING-LEFT: 20px; WIDTH: 199px; HEIGHT: 191px" border="0" align="right" src="https://www.dropbox.com/scl/fi/1f38g5kqd25vqmgwbuuhu/brokenchessboard3.jpg?rlkey=dxhpzw3rtwgf3dlpml4xncg7v&raw=1" width="199" height="191">Abstract: </strong>For any partition of a positive integer we consider the chess (or draughts) colouring of its associated Ferrers graph. Let b denote the total number of black unit squares, and w the number of white squares. In this note we characterise all pairs (b,w) which arise in this way. This simple combinatorical result was discovered by characterising Hilbert series of certain right modules over cubic three dimensional Artin-Schelter algebras. However in this note we present a purely combinatorical proof. 
</p><p>The result is (at least partially) known, see <a href="http://www.maths.usyd.edu.au/u/SUMS/" name=""><em>Sydney University Mathematical Society Problems </em></a>Competition 2004, problem 10. However 
we found it interesting to present an alternative proof. All additional references and remarks will be mostly appreciated.
   </p></li>
</ol>
</div>

<h3><font size="3">Programma's in Maple</font></h3>

<ol>
   <li>
      The maple worksheet <a href="http://www.freewebs.com/koendenaeghel/~lucp1324/enumeration1.mws" name="">enumeration1.mws</a> allows us to enumerate various data mentioned in the article <em>Ideal classes of three dimensional Artin-Schelter regular algebras</em>, such as lists of Castelnuovo polynomials, Hilbert series, graded Betti numbers and minimal resolutions, dimensions of strata, etc. 
   </li>
   <li>
      The first part of maple worksheet <a href="http://www.freewebs.com/koendenaeghel/enumeration2.mws" name="">enumeration2.mws</a> lists elementary data in relation with the submitted paper <em>On incidence between strata of the Hilbert scheme of points on P^2</em>. Thus it may be considered as the commutative version of 1. With help of the second part of this worksheet one is able to solve explicit incidence problems between strata on the Hilbert scheme. For quick additional background we refer to the talks "On the incidence between strata of the Hilbert scheme of points on the projective plane". A quicker version of this worksheet - though without examples - is <a href="http://alpha.luc.ac.be/~lucp1324/enumeration2quick.mws" name="">enumeration2quick.mws</a>. With help of this improved version we have checked the equivalence of Condition B and C (see submitted paper) for 1 &lt;= n &lt;= 70. 
   </li>
</ol>
